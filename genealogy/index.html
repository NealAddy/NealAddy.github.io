Addy / Dillard Family Genealogy


The White Rabbit put on his spectacles.  `Where shall I begin, please your Majesty?' he asked.   `Begin at the beginning,' the King said gravely, `and go on till you come to the end: then stop.'

Introduction

Upon approaching this project I asked myself the same question that the White Rabbit had asked the King.  Where does one begin?  If only we could take the King's advice and "begin at the beginning".  If only genealogy were that easy.
As with any genealogical record this work has neither a beginning nor an end.  The questions of beginnings are actually the easiest part of genealogy.  Their answer is simple: there are none.  The larger question in my mind concerned the end:  Where do I stop?
Many surnames are represented here but the primary focus of my research has been on two lines:   1) William Addy, born in Ireland around 1750; and 2) James Dillard, born in Virginia during the mid-1700's.  These are my direct ancestors through the paternal lines of my parents.  Both lines, of course, are from much larger families than represented here and where possible I have included branches out to at least one or two generations from the primary branches that are the scope of this work.  This has been done primarily to provide reference points for other genealogists researching other lines of these families.
Other prominent surnames in this database include Carter, Harrell, McCann, Peacock, Sewell, and Shaver.   Geographical points of interest include the panhandle of Florida (particularly Calhoun and Jackson Counties), Arkansas (Pulaski and Saline Counties), Texas (Bowie County), and to a lesser extent, South Carolina (Edgefield County), and Georgia (Monroe County).  My current research is attempting to focus on the Dillards of Henry County, Halifax County and Pittsylvania County in Virginia.  I would be especially interested in hearing from folks who are also researching this region.
At this time there are 626 families in the database comprised of 1720 individuals.
Note:  This is very much a work in progress, both from a genealogical research and website development perspective.  Please check back now and then to see the latest changes.
